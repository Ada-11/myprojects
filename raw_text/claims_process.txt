1. Process Overview
This document outlines the standard operating procedures for submitting, reviewing, and adjudicating health insurance claims. Follow these steps to ensure compliance, minimize processing delays, and prevent claim denials.

2. Step-by-Step Workflow
Step 1: Claim Intake & Formatting
Electronic claims must be submitted via the standard EDI 837 format.
Paper claims must use the official, original red-ink CMS-1500 or UB-04 forms.
Black-and-white photocopies of paper forms will be automatically rejected by the optical character recognition (OCR) scanner. [1, 2]
Step 2: Member and Provider Verification
Match the patient’s Member ID, group number, and date of birth against the active enrollment registry.
Cross-reference the billing provider's National Provider Identifier (NPI) to confirm network participation status.
Step 3: Medical Adjudication
Validate that all diagnostic codes (ICD-10-CM) align logically with the billed procedure codes (CPT/HCPCS).
Review the patient's plan files to calculate deductible tracking, co-insurance percentages, and out-of-pocket maximums. [1]
Step 4: Final Settlement & Notification
Generate the final payment or issue a formal denial notice within 30 business days of initial receipt.
Distribute the Explanation of Benefits (EOB) statement directly to the member.
3. Claim Auditor Checklist
The processing agent must verify and check off each item before finalizing adjudication:
Patient Demographics: Name matches the insurance card exactly.
Prior Authorization: Active authorization number is attached for inpatient or specialized services.
Timely Filing Window: Claim was submitted within 180 days of the date of service.
Coordination of Benefits (COB): Primary insurance payment details are attached if this plan is secondary.
Code Integrity: No unbundled CPT codes or invalid modifiers are present. [1, 2]
